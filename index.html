Batangueño Dictionary

Term: Baliskog

Meaning: Curved, bent

Example: "Ang kalsada ay baliskog." (The road is curved.)

Phonetic: /ba-lis-kog/

Term: Amba

Meaning: To signal, to gesture

Example: "Nag-amba siya na aalis na." (He signaled that he was about to leave.)

Phonetic: /am-ba/

History of Batangueño Terms

Term: Bahala Na

The phrase "Bahala na" is deeply rooted in the Filipino psyche, particularly among Batangueños. It traces its origins to pre-colonial times, where "Bathala" (a deity) was invoked, entrusting one's fate to a higher power. Over time, it evolved into "Bahala na," reflecting a resilient and adaptive mindset in the face of uncertainty.

Term: Kumusta

Derived from the Spanish "¿Cómo está?" during the colonial period, "Kumusta" has been adapted into the Batangueño dialect as a common greeting. It symbolizes the fusion of local and foreign influences that have shaped the language over centuries.
